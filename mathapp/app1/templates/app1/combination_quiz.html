Info Sheet

Question 1: A basket has 2 yellow cubes, 4 blue cubes and 5 red cubes. In how many ways can a person pick 3 cubes?


Hint: Don't overthink the question, the colors don't really effect the answer

Question 2: How many ways can you make a three card hand from a standard deck of 52 cards such that exactly two of the cards are not face cards?


Hint: The key phrase is NOT face cards

Question 3: You are picking numbers out of a hat that range from 1 through 50 inclusive. You are to select 5 numbers, and no numbers can be repeated. In how many ways can you select the numbers so that you have at least one number larger than 20?


Hint: Use the indirect method

Question 4: You are given the following two sets: A = {blue, white, black}, B = {white, yellow}. What is the value of n(A U B)?


Hint: A U B means the elements in A or B

Question 5: How many terms are there in the binomial expansion of

(2x - y)^{12}

Hint: How many numbers are there in the 12th row of Pascal's triangle?

Question 6: How many ways can you draw a spade or a face card from a standard deck of cards?


Hint: Remember not to double count

Question 7: A jury of 12 people is to selected from a group of 14 men and 10 women. How many ways can the jury be selected if there must be at least 2 women on the jury?


Hint: Use indirect method

Question 8: You are given the following two sets: X = {1,3,5,7,9} Y = {0,2,4,6,8}. What is the set given by X and Y?


Hint: X and Y means the elements that X and Y have in common

Question 9: What is the value of the coefficient of the fifth power of a in the binomial expansion given by

(a-3)^{6}

Hint: You only need to find the second term in the expansion

Question 10: How many 5 card poker hands dealt from a standard deck of cards have exactly 3 red cards and exactly 2 spades?


Hint: How many red cards are there? How many spades are there?

Question 11: There is a box containing 6 red balls, 3 yellow balls and 4 green balls. In how many ways can you select three balls and have either exactly 2 be red or at least 2 be green?


Hint: These are disjoint cases so you will have to add them up

Question 12: You roll 3 dice. How many ways can you roll them such that exactly 2 of the numbers are prime?


Hint: Each dice has 3 prime numbers on it
