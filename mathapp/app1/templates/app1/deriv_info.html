Calculate Derivatives

Definitions

In your functions courses you will recall the concept of the average rate of change of some function f(x). In that context, we refer to the average rate of change between two point on the function, and compute it as the slope of the secant line connecting those two points:

AROC = \frac{f(x_{2}) - f(x_{1})}{x_{2}-x_{1}}

Now let h = x2 - x1. Making this substitution, the above formula can be re written as

AROC = \frac{f(x_{1} + h) - f(x_{1})}{h}

Below is a visual representation of the secant line for the function

f(x) = x^{3} - 2x + 5

from the point x = -1 to x = 1

As we make x1 closer and closer to x2, we are making their difference approach 0. Or in other words, we are taking the limit of the slope of the secant as h goes to 0. Visually, this looks like

The closer x1 gets to x2 (in this case, x = 1), the more the secant line begins to look like a tangent line. In fact, if we take the limit as h goes to 0 in our previous formula, we precisely obtain the slope of the tangent line at x1. This is the definition of the derivative of f(x) at the point x1:

f ' (x_{1}) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x_{1} + h) - f(x_{1})}{h}

Where f'(x) is the notation that means "the derivative of f at x". Using this formula to calculate derivatives is sometimes referred to as finding the derivative by "first principles".

Derivative Shortcuts

Below are a list of rules that can be used to quickly evaluate certain types of derivatives


Power Rule

f(x) = x^{n} \\ \phantom{aaa} f \ ' (x) = nx^{n-1}

Product Rule

h(x) = f(x)g(x) \\ \phantom{aaa} h \ ' (x) = f \ '(x)g(x) + f(x)g \ '(x)

Quotient Rule

h(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \\ \\ \phantom{aaa} h \ ' (x) = \frac{f \ '(x)g(x) - f(x)g \ '(x)}{[g(x)]^{2}}

Chain Rule

h(x) = f[g(x)] \\ \phantom{aaa} h \ '(x) = f \ '[g(x)]g \ '(x)

As an example of the chain rule, consider the following function

h(x) = \sqrt{x^{3} - 2x + 5}

The functions f and g are

f(x) = \sqrt{x} \\ \phantom{aaa} g(x) = x^{3} - 2x + 5

Since indeed, f[g(x)] = h(x) by composition of functions. The derivatives of f and g are given by

f \ '(x) = \frac{1}{2}x^{-\frac{1}{2}} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \\ \\ \phantom{aaa} g \ '(x) = 3x^{2} - 2

And so, the derivative of h(x) is

h \ '(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x^{3} - 2x + 5}} (3x^{2} - 2) \\ \\ \phantom{aaa} \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ = \frac{3x^{2} - 2}{2\sqrt{x^{3} - 2x + 5}}
