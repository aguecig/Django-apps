Question 1: There are five people walking to the gates of an amusement park. How many ways can they pass through the front gate, one person at a time?


Hint: Use a factorial

Question 2: In how many ways can a 7 digit phone number be created if the phone number cannot start with a 0?


Hint: How many possibilites are there for each digit in the number?

Question 3: There are six people on the student council and the council is to select a president and vice president. Alexis and Sofia refuse to serve together as either president or vice president. In how many ways can the council select the two members to lead?


Hint: Try using the indirect method

Question 4: A particular lock combination has 3 numbers that range from 0 to 39. How many combinations are possible if two consecutive numbers in the combination cannot be identical?


Hint: The first number in the combination is unrestricted

Question 5: How many ways can a group of 8 people be lined up if Justin and Zack must be side by side in the line?


Hint: Consider Zack and Justin to be one person in the line up

Question 6: In how many ways can a club and an ace be selected from a standard deck of cards without replacement?


Hint: What happens if the club chosen is an ace?

Question 7: How many 5 digit odd numbers are there that can neither start with a 3 nor end with a 1?


Hint: Use the slot method and determine how many possibilities there are for each digit

Question 8: How many 3 digit numbers can begin with a 2 or end with a 5?


Hint: Remember that or means to add the two cases together

Question 9: In how many different ways can the letters in the wird mississippi be arranged?


Hint: How do we acccount for repeated characters?

Question 10: How many numbers are there in the thirteenth row of Pascal's triangle?


Hint: Draw out the first few rows and see if you can find the pattern

Question 12: Which of the following is equivalent to 8P3?


Hint: You could just calculate each one, or use the permutation formula

Question 13: In how many ways can 4 people arrive at a party if Alice must show up before Bob and Charlie must show up either first or last?



Hint: Do it case by case

Question 14: In how many different ways can the letters A, A, B, B, B, C, D, E be arranged if the letter C must be to the right of the letter D?


Hint: You need to consider repeated elements, as well as the cases where C does not follow D.

Question 15: If you have 9 books, in how many ways can you pick out 8 books in alphabetical order?


Hint: Once you pick 8 books, how many ways can you arrange them to meet the criteria?

Question 16: At a certain highschool, there are three math classes for the grade 12 students: Functions, Calculus and Data. In the first semester, 7 students are taking all three classes. 16 students are taking both Calculus and Data, 22 students are taking functions and data and 18 students are taking calculus and functions. 49 students are enrolled in calculus, 64 students are enrolled in functions and 68 students are enrolled in data. How many total students are enrolled in at least one of the math courses this semester?


Hint: Make a venn diagram
