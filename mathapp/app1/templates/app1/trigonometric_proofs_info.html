List of Trigonometric Identities

\tan{x} = \frac{ \sin{x} }{ \cos{x} }, \quad \cot {x} = \frac{ \cos{x} }{ \sin{x} } \\ \\ \csc{x} = \frac{ 1 }{ \sin{x} }, \quad \sec{x} = \frac{ 1 }{ \cos{x} } \\ \\ \sin{^{2}x} + \cos{^{2}x} = 1 \\ \\ 1 - \sin{^{2}x} = \cos{^{2}x}, \quad 1 - \cos{^{2}x} = \sin{^{2}x} \\ \\

Useful Strategies for Trigonometric Proofs

  1. Decide which side of the equation looks easier to manipulate for yourself. This is often the side that has more trigonometric functions and terms, but not always. Keep in mind that there is no correct side, as they can both be manipulated to look like the other. But more often than not, once side is easier to work with than the other.
  2. Re-write the side you chose (without the equal sign) when you begin your proof.
  3. Look for trigonometric identities to use that will simplify the side you chose.
  4. Look for any algebraic techniques you can use that may help create simplifications to the proof. This could be along the lines of factoring, creating common denominators, distributing, etc.
  5. Continue to look for trig identities and algebraic techniques until the proof is completed. Remember there are many ways to prove a trig identity, and they will vary in length. There is no one correct way.
