On this page you can practise naming molecules from organic chemistry. The section will be periodically updated as the semester goes on (September 2019)

Keep in mind that the answers will require perfect grammatical syntax in order to be correct. Here are some examples:

  • 2,3-dimethylhexane would be considered correct, but 2,3 dimethylhexane would not, since it is missing the -
  • pentan-1-ene would be correct, but Pentan-1-ene would not since it contains a captial letter
  • 3-ethylnonane is correct, but 3-ethyl nonane is not because it contains a space

Legend for color coded atoms

Hydrogen = Blue

Carbon = Black

Oxygen = Grey

Nitrogen = Orange
